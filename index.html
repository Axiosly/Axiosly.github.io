Teaching Course & Supervision

  • User Experience Design

    This course equips students with skills needed for entry-level UX design jobs, focusing on making everyday interactions usable, enjoyable, and accessible.

    2024/2025 S2

    User Experience Design is the first of a series of Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) courses that will equip students with the skills needed to apply to entry-level jobs in user experience (UX) design. UX designers focus on the interactions that people have with products like websites, mobile apps, and physical objects. UX designers make those everyday interactions usable, enjoyable, and accessible.

    The role of an entry-level UX designer might include empathizing with users, defining their pain points, coming up with ideas for design solutions, creating wireframes, prototypes, and mockups, and testing designs to get feedback.

    By the end of this course, students will be able to:

    • Define the field of UX and explain why it's important for consumers and businesses.
    • Understand foundational concepts in UX design, such as user-centered design, the design thinking framework, accessibility, and equity-focused design.
    • Identify the factors that contribute to great user experience design.
    • Explain why design sprints are an important and useful part of a UX designer's work.
    • Describe common UX research methods.
    • Identify and account for biases in UX research.

    By completing this course, students will gain a solid foundation in UX principles and practices, preparing them for further study and entry-level roles in this rapidly growing field.

  • User Requirement Research and Analysis

    This course focuses on how to interact with users to understand what they need or expect, and examine their behaviors and preformance.

    2024/2025 S1

    User Requirement Research and Analysis is a UX course that focuses on how to interact with users (or potential users) to understand what they need, what they currently do, what they love and hate, and examine human capabilities and behavior as they relate to UX design. Designing effective interactive systems requires understanding the needs and capabilities of the people who will be using them.

    Learners will be introduced to numerous techniques to gather data from and about users. The course covers an end-to-end methodology for qualitative research that is specifically suited for understanding user needs.

    While qualitative methods provide deep contextual insights, quantitative analysis plays an equally critical role in user requirements research. This course introduces students to quantitative approaches for complementing qualitative findings, including the design and analysis of structured surveys, descriptive and inferential statistical techniques (e.g., mean, standard deviation, t-tests, chi-square tests), and the interpretation of behavioral data collected through analytics tools and logging. Students will learn to apply a mixed-methods perspective, integrating quantitative metrics with qualitative narratives to derive more robust and actionable user requirements.

    Students will learn:

    • Find out what user needs assessments are, what qualitative and quantitative research is, and how these are related.
    • Learn an end-to-end methodology for qualitative and quantitative research that is suited for understanding user needs.
    • Be exposed to good practices for preforming statistic analysis and conducting semi-structured interviews, in-situ observation, and affinity walls.
    • Gain some hand-on experience with semi-structured interviews, in-situ observations, and affinity walls.

    By completing this course, students will develop practical skills in user research that are essential for evidence-based UX design and decision-making.

  • Augmented Reality Design

    This course covers the augmented reality (AR) experiences and games for commercial release using ARCore and other industry tools.

    2025/2026 S1/S2

    Augmented Reality Design covers the fundamental concepts of XR, how to design and develop XR applications, and how to bring XR into instructional settings. This AR course explores the exciting world of Unity development, focusing on creating mixed reality experiences and preparing games for commercial release. Students will learn to build mobile and immersive AR games while mastering the technical and business aspects of game development.

    Through hands-on projects, students will develop essential skills in XR interaction, environment design, and implementing complex game mechanics. The course provides practical knowledge to create engaging, interactive games optimized for both AR and VR platforms.

    This class covers the fundamentals of augmented reality (AR) and how to build an AR experience using ARCore. Students will learn:

    • How to identify different types of AR experiences.
    • Tools and platforms used in the AR landscape.
    • What makes AR feel "real".
    • Popular use cases for AR.
    • How to create an AR use flow.
    • How AR experiences work.
    • Tools like Google Poly and Unity to build AR experiences.
    • Next steps to start building an AR experience using ARCore and other tools.

    This course will break down complex AR concepts to make them easy to understand, while also sharing expert tips and knowledge from industry practitioners.

  • Virtual Reality Design

    This course explores the fundamental concepts of immersive interaction, focusing on designing VR experiences using game engines and Toolkits.

    2024/2025 S1

    Virtual Reality Design covers the fundamental concepts of XR, how to design and develop XR applications, how to discuss the emerging key issues in the landscape of XR, and how to bring XR into instructional settings. This VR course explores the exciting world of Unity development, focusing on creating immersive virtual reality experiences and preparing applications for commercial release. Students will learn to build immersive VR applications while mastering the technical and business aspects of VR development.

    Through hands-on projects, students will develop essential skills in XR interaction, environment design, and implementing complex interaction mechanics. The course provides practical knowledge to create engaging, interactive experiences optimized for VR platforms.

    This class covers the fundamentals of virtual reality (VR) interaction and how to build immersive VR experiences using tools like the VR Interaction Toolkit, OpenXR, and SteamVR. Students will learn:

    • How to identify different types of VR interactions and why they differ from traditional screen-based apps.
    • Tools and platforms (e.g., VRChat) used in the VR landscape for creating natural, immersive interactions.
    • What makes VR interaction feel natural and intuitive – from body tracking to haptic feedback.
    • Popular use cases for VR interaction, such as gaming, training, simulation, and collaborative workspaces.
    • How to design a VR interaction flow, including locomotion, selection, and manipulation of virtual objects.
    • How VR interactions work in Unity – tracking, input mapping, and real-time rendering.
    • Toolkit like OpenXR and SteamVR to build and prototype interactive VR experiences.
    • Next steps to start building VR interactions using these frameworks.

    This course will break down complex VR interaction concepts to make them easy to understand. Students will get hands-on practice through a final project by designing and implementing VR interactions from start to finish.

  • BUXR Lab

    We are interested in designing, developing, and evaluating user interfaces and interaction techniques to help users solve their daily issues.

    2025-2026

    BUXR Lab, i.e., the Bridge User eXperience & Reality Lab, is a human-computer interaction laboratory dedicated to user-centered design, cross-platform immersive experiences, and research in extended reality (XR), data visualization, and artificial intelligence. We firmly believe that the value of technology lies in serving and empowering users. BUXR Lab is a pioneering interdisciplinary community that converges diverse expertise, integrates cutting-edge technologies, and strives to bridge the gap between humans and the digital world.

    🌉 Bridge — Our Core Mission

    Our core mission is to establish a bridge between users and interactive products and services. We bridge:

    • Reality and Experience: exploring XR, the metaverse, and digital twins to seamlessly integrate digital experiences into real life.
    • User and System: transforming complex technologies into intuitive, satisfying, and efficient interfaces and workflows.
    • Research and Application: translating cutting-edge academic theories into real-world products, services, and solutions.

    🔬 eXperience & Reality — Our Focus

    We mainly focus on both dimensions:

    • User Experience (UX): investigating human behavior and cognition to design user interactions and interfaces.
    • Extended Reality (XR): integrating advanced technologies to create interactive environments.

    Current Research and Practice

    BUXR Lab engages in the following frontier research domains:

    • Immersive Interaction: developing novel gesture-based interactions, spatial computing, and environmental perception systems for VR/AR devices.
    • Multimodal Affective Computing: studying user experience in complex human-machine systems through eye-tracking, EEG, and other physiological signals, combined with quantitative analysis methods to derive data-driven insights.
    • Interface Prototyping: building human-machine interfaces (HMI) such as intelligent voice, haptic, and interactive installations.
    • User Research and Data Analytics: employing scientific methodologies — including both qualitative and quantitative approaches — to evaluate and optimize the efficiency and satisfaction of human-machine interaction in existing products and systems.

    🚀 We Are Looking For

    We are passionate about user psychology, emerging technologies, and innovative design, and we strive to transform cold code and hardware into warm, human-centered experiences. At BUXR Lab, members will find a community of like-minded peers, working together with intelligence and craftsmanship to shape the future of human-computer interaction.

    Fragrance Journey: Integrating Virtual Reality and Electroencephalography Signals for Perfume Customization

    Authors: Zirui Yu, Yanze Gao, Hongyu Ji, Cancan Jin, Yiping Zhang, Ningning Xu*

    Conference: CHI EA '26: Proceedings of the Extended Abstracts of the 2026 CHI Conference on Human Factors in Computing Systems

    Abstract: Selecting perfume is often a challenging task that relies on fleeting impressions, making it difficult for customers to compare scents or articulate their preferences. In this paper, we present a data-driven co-creation system that integrates virtual reality (VR), scent-delivery installations, electroencephalography (EEG) data, and artificial intelligence (AI) generated cards with augmented reality (AR) support to transform the concrete experience into a quantified assessment for perfume-making. In this Fragrance Journey, users explore the scenario-based VR environment to sample scents, providing explicit ratings while the system simultaneously captures EEG-derived features. These physiological indicators are converted into weighted metrics, which an algorithm uses to synthesize high-scoring components across fragrance notes into a personalized formula, and then visualized as a unique AI-generated card to get perfume profiles for follow-up customization. The proposed design integrates digital contexts with physical olfactory stimuli, offering a novel paradigm that would contribute to the customization of scent products.

    VRhythmSpace: Designing a Musical Game for Play, Learning, and Performance

    Authors: Jiawen Li, Zheyuan Jiang, Yifei Chen, Yuwei Ren, Jingji Li, Kai Yao, Xu Sun, Bin Jia*, Ningning Xu*

    Conference: IEEE International Conference on Educational and Information Technology (ICEIT 2026)

    Abstract: Virtual Reality (VR) is an emerging technique that alters the way users interact with music content. In this paper, we introduce VRhythmSpace, a VR musical game prototype that enables users to engage, learn, and play with music.     LanternOperAR: Engaging Yue Opera Culture with Chinese Philosophy in Hybrid Gift through Interactive Experience

    Authors: Ningning Xu, Yifei Chen, Teng Ma, Qinling Mao, Xinlie Huang, Haibo Zhou, Xu Sun*

    Journal: ACM Journal on Computing and Cultural Heritage (JOCCH)

    Abstract: Yue Opera is a traditional Chinese through-sung storytelling style that conveys Chinese cultural ideologies and historical depth. In this context, Augmented Reality (AR) has been adopted in cultural venues to break the passive watching experience, turning the audience into active participants. In this article, we present an exploratory case study of LanternOperAR, a hybrid cultural gift to promote Yue Opera with the Chinese philosophy of Yangmingism. We incorporate the tangible interface and virtual content to create an interactive cultural experience. Specifically, we conducted a fieldwork study to investigate the promises and challenges of promoting Yue Opera. Based on expert interviews and an online survey with the public, we derived a set of design goals to guide the design and development process. Our approach incorporates the application of AI image processing, generative AI, information visualization, and gamification with AR technology to generate and visualize cultural knowledge in AR-based narratives and interactions. We evaluated LanternOperAR with 20 participants and acquired some positive feedback on system usability and overall experience, as well as some suggestions for further improvements. Our findings provide insights into the design of cultural products and cultural applications as guidance for cultural researchers and practitioners.

    Authors: Hongyu Ji, Cancan Jin, Ningning Xu*, Yiping Zhang*

    Conference: International Symposium on Visual Information Communication and Interaction (VINCI 2025)

    Abstract: As a hot topic in the 21 century, cultural digitization is an important research spot in both the cultural industry and educational field, providing new opportunities to engage in cultural heritage. In this context, the hybrid cultural gift, with the integration of tangible interfaces and digital technologies, is a promising step toward a more engaging, personalized, and socialized experience for cultural inheritance.     Guidelines for combining user preference and interaction experience to visualize spatial imagination in VR memory training

    Authors: Cancan Jin, Yanze Gao, Zirui Yu, Ningning Xu*

    Conference: 2025 IEEE International Symposium on Mixed and Augmented Reality Adjunct (ISMAR-Adjunct)

    Abstract: Memory is an essential part of life. A comprehensive grasp of mnemonic techniques enables the application of effective memory strategies and will enhance educational and professional outcomes. The Method of Loci is widely recognized as an effective scientific mnemonic technique, though it has certain limitations due to its high demand for spatial imagination. In this paper, we conducted an exploratory comparative study (N = 36) to investigate the relationship between three mnemonic methods (i.e., Loci Booklet, Personal Adapted Loci, LociVR) and memory performance and experience. We recorded subjective ratings of user experience, measured times, test scores, and memory retention in this study. The quantitative and qualitative analyzes provide design directions for memory training based on mnemonic methods. We further identified the design goals from the preliminary investigation, and proposed key design features, as well as interactive mechanisms, through a participatory workshop (N = 28). Based on these findings, we derived a set of design implications. In future, the design of LociVR would be optimized and contribute to a more personalized and interactive experience for memory training.

    LivehouseVR: Creating an Immersive and Inclusive Social VR Experience for DJs and Audiences towards Gender Equality

    Authors: Kaiwen Li, Shuyan Chen, Yifei Cai, Yifei Chen, Ningning Xu*

    Conference: CHI EA '25: Proceedings of the Extended Abstracts of the CHI Conference on Human Factors in Computing Systems

    Abstract: Gender equality is a fundamental human right, essential for building a peaceful, prosperous, and sustainable community. Despite the progress made over the last decades, old-fashioned sexist attitudes are still common in many professional fields, including DJing. Virtual Reality offers a potential solution for reducing bias by hiding the biological gender identity, thereby shifting the public focus from human features and stereotypes to DJ’s quality. In this paper, we design and curate an interactive live house event in VRChat to explore the role of avatar gender in shaping social interactions and performer identity in DJ performances. Additionally, we integrate a rich array of features to support seamless interaction between the real and virtual worlds in live house events. Preliminary findings from user feedback indicated that our design solution has promising potential to promote gender equality and inclusive interaction. This contributes to a more equitable DJing community aligned with sustainable development goals.

    CubeMuseum AR: A Tangible Augmented Reality Interface for Cultural Heritage Learning and Museum Gifting

    User Retention of Mobile Augmented Reality for Cultural Heritage Learning

    Authors: Ningning Xu, Yue Li*, Jie Lin, Lingyun Yu, Hai-Ning Liang

    Conference: 2022 IEEE International Symposium on Mixed and Augmented Reality Adjunct (ISMAR-Adjunct)

    Abstract: Mobile Augmented Reality (AR) is becoming increasingly affordable and popular with the constantly improving computing power of mobile devices and the popularity of smartphones and tablets. In this paper, we present a user study that investigates user retention of mobile AR in cultural heritage learning. We developed a mobile AR application that allows users to observe 3D models of museum artifacts and learn about their culture and history. Participants achieved a knowledge retention rate of 78.21%, indicating the positive effects of mobile AR on cultural heritage learning. We performed a structural equation modeling analysis (N=50) to investigate the effects of usability, satisfaction, emotional attachment, focus of attention, and flow experience on user retention of mobile AR. The analysis results confirmed that user satisfaction and flow experience positively affect user retention. Usability and focus of attention contribute positively to user satisfaction and flow experience respectively.
